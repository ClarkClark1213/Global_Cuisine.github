KIMCHI

Traditional Napa Cabbage Kimchi Recipe Prep Time: 2 hrs (plus fermentation) | Cook Time: 0 | Total Time: 2+ days Servings: 2 quarts


Ingredients:

Main Vegetable:
1 large napa cabbage (about 4 lbs)
Brine:
½ cup kosher salt
8 cups water
Porridge:
2 tbsp sweet rice flour
1 cup water
1 tbsp sugar
Kimchi Paste:
1 cup Korean red pepper flakes (gochugaru)
½ cup fish sauce
¼ cup salted shrimp (saeujeot)
1 small onion
8 garlic cloves
1-inch ginger piece
1 tbsp salted shrimp brine
Vegetable Mix-ins:
1 daikon radish, julienned
4 scallions, cut into 2-inch pieces
1 carrot, julienned (optional)

Instructions:

1.Prepare Cabbage:
Cut cabbage lengthwise into quarters. Soak in brine for 1½ hours, turning every 30 mins.
2.Make Porridge:
Mix rice flour and water, cook until thickened. Add sugar, cool.
3.Prepare Paste:
Blend onion, garlic, ginger with fish sauce and shrimp into smooth paste.
Combine with cooled porridge and gochugaru.
4.Combine Vegetables:
Rinse brined cabbage thoroughly. Mix with radish, scallions and paste.
5.Pack Jars:
Pack tightly into clean jars, pressing to remove air bubbles.
6.Ferment:
Leave at room temperature 1-2 days, then refrigerate.
7.Serve:
Ready after 1 week; best after 2-4 weeks.

Tips:
✔ Use Korean chili flakes (gochugaru) for authentic flavor
✔ Wear gloves when mixing - chili will stain hands
✔ Burp jars daily during fermentation
✔ Ideal fermentation: 65-70°F (18-21°C)
✔ Vegan option: Replace fish sauce with 3 tbsp miso + ½ cup mushroom broth
✔ Serving ideas: Kimchi jjigae, fried rice, or pancakes
✔ Storage: Lasts 3-6 months refrigerated
